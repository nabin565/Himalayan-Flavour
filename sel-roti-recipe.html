Sel Roti (Nepalese Rice Bread)

Sel Roti is a traditional, ring-shaped, slightly sweet bread made from rice flour. It's a delicacy prepared during the major Nepalese festivals of Dashain and Tihar. It has a unique crispy yet chewy texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice (Jasmine or any regular rice, not Basmati)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (or to taste)
  • 3 tbsp ghee (clarified butter), melted
  • 1/4 cup rice flour (to adjust consistency)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder (optional)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water overnight, or for at least 4-5 hours.
  2. Create the batter: Drain the soaked rice completely. In a blender, combine the drained rice, sugar, and melted ghee. Blend until you get a smooth, thick batter. The consistency should be like a thick pancake batter. If it's too thin, add a little rice flour. If it's too thick, add a tablespoon of water. Stir in the cardamom powder if using. Let the batter rest for about 30 minutes.
  3. Heat the oil: Pour about 2-3 inches of oil into a deep, wide pan (karahi) and heat it over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
  4. Shape and fry the roti: This step requires practice. Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle or a piping bag. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a continuous circular motion to form a large ring. You can also do this traditionally by hand.
  5. Fry for about 1-2 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip it using long wooden or metal skewers/sticks and fry the other side for another minute.
  6. Drain and serve: Once both sides are golden brown, lift the Sel Roti out of the oil with the sticks, letting excess oil drip off. Place it on a wire rack or paper towels to cool.
  7. Serve as a snack on its own, or with yogurt or pickles.
