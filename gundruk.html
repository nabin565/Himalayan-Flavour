Authentic Gundruk Soup

Gundruk is one of Nepal's most distinctive and beloved dishes. It is a fermented leafy green vegetable, and its sour, tangy soup (Gundruk ko Jhol) is a perfect appetizer or a comforting meal with rice.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup dried Gundruk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise (optional)
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried gundruk in warm water for about 15-20 minutes until it softens. Squeeze out the excess water and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the turmeric and cumin powder. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and pulpy.
  5. Add the soaked gundruk to the pan. Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Pour in the water or vegetable broth, add the green chilies (if using), and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  8. Season with salt. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice.
